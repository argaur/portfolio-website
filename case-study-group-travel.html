Overview

Executive Summary

Group travel planning is a $168.7 billion global market growing at 7.2% CAGR — faster than solo travel — yet the coordination layer that helps groups decide, plan, and execute trips together remains almost entirely unsolved.

Today, groups stitch together 4–5 disconnected tools (WhatsApp, Google Sheets, Splitwise, Airbnb, Google Maps) and one person absorbs the entire coordination burden. The result: the organiser has the worst trip experience, silent misalignments around budget and diet surface mid-trip as conflict, and 2–3 weeks of planning inactivity kill group energy before departure.

The core insight from 6 user interviews and deep secondary research: group travel planning is fundamentally a social and emotional challenge, not a logistical one. The hardest part isn’t finding hotels — it’s getting six people to agree on anything without social awkwardness.

03 — Archetypes

User Archetypes

Four distinct archetypes emerged from interview analysis. Most users exhibit more than one archetype depending on group composition and trip type.

The Delegator Approver

Takes no active lead; participates in validation. Waits for 2–3 options to be shortlisted, then picks one. Voices dissatisfaction after the fact if not consulted before final booking.

The Reluctant Organiser

Becomes de-facto planner by default — due to local knowledge, seniority, or history. Books accommodation, manages expenses, drives the car. Bears disproportionate cognitive load.

The Budget Whisperer

Acutely aware of group financial dynamics. Silently calibrates preferences to implied budget. Uses Airbnb price selection as an indirect budget signal. No socially safe mechanism to communicate limits.

The Experience-First Traveller with Constraints

Wants a rich trip but constrained by family needs — young kids, dietary restrictions, physical limitations. Filters heavily for kitchen access, stroller-friendliness, kid-friendly itineraries.

04 — Findings

Cross-Cutting Themes

Validated across 4+ of 6 interviews:

T1 — WhatsApp + Spreadsheet Cobbling

All 6 interviewees used WhatsApp as primary coordination. Critical decisions are scattered across chat threads, Google Docs, booking confirmations, and Splitwise. No single source of truth.

T2 — Budget is the First Conversation but the Hardest to Have

Budget discussion is universally acknowledged as the starting point — yet groups consistently avoid stating actual constraints. Mismatch surfaces during the trip as resentment, not pre-trip as planning input.

T3 — Dietary & Preference Misalignment Discovered Too Late

6/6 interviews referenced dietary restrictions as a coordination failure. Ranges from veg/non-veg to Jain food, onion/garlic-free, and kid-friendly needs. No current tool prompts or collects this information.

T4 — Planning Load is Asymmetric

In every trip described, 1–2 people did the majority of research, booking, and coordination. Others are “along for the ride” during planning, then vocal during execution. Creates organiser resentment.

T5 — Sub-Group Divergence During Trips

4/6 interviewees described instances where the group naturally split during the trip. This is viewed positively — but is entirely unplanned and managed ad hoc via WhatsApp.

T6 — Splitwise as a Standard, but Effortful

Splitwise mentioned by 3/6 as the go-to expense tracker. Pain: manual entry is tedious; people forget or stop logging. Best workaround: split fixed costs upfront so Splitwise only handles variable expenses.

Critical Gaps — What No Tool Solves

Anonymous budget preference collection

No tool creates a socially safe mechanism for budget disclosure. Groups proxy preferences through accommodation price selection.

Upfront dietary / constraint onboarding

No tool prompts or collects dietary restrictions, mobility needs, or kids’ requirements before planning begins.

Single group dashboard / source of truth

No product shows: where are we going, when, what’s the plan each day, what’s the cost, what’s confirmed.

07 — Problem

The Three Root Problems

Root Problem 1: Silent Budget Misalignment

Budget is the #1 source of group trip friction and the least openly discussed topic. No tool creates a “socially safe” mechanism for budget disclosure. The mismatch surfaces mid-trip as resentment, not pre-trip as planning input. This is not a problem of dishonesty — it is a structural design gap.

Root Problem 2: The Organiser Tax

1–2 people absorb 80%+ of all coordination load — research, booking, reminders, expense tracking, and live itinerary management. This creates a single point of failure before and during the trip. The irony: the organiser consistently has the worst trip experience.

Root Problem 3: No Single Source of Truth

Information is scattered across WhatsApp threads, Google Docs, booking confirmation emails, Instagram saves, Notes apps, and Splitwise. No single page shows the full picture. Every interaction adds friction; no interaction builds shared context.

Core Insight: Group travel coordination is a fundamentally social and emotional challenge — not merely a logistical one. Users avoid direct conversations about money and preferences, leading to silent misalignments that surface during the trip itself and degrade the collective experience.
