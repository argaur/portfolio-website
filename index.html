05 — HOW I THINK

Systems over features.
Always.

I approach product work the way an architect approaches a building — first understand the forces acting on the system, then design the structure. Features are load-bearing walls, not decoration.

01

Systems Over Features

Features are the visible 10%. The system underneath — data flow, decision rights, escalation paths — is the 90% that decides whether the feature ships value or noise.

02

Compress Complexity, Don’t Hide It

Good products don’t pretend the underlying problem is simple; they make complexity navigable. The best interfaces expose the right three of the fourteen edge cases — deliberately.

03

AI Reduces Operational Entropy

AI is most valuable where humans repeat structured judgment under fatigue: screening, triage, routing, summarisation. It earns its place when entropy goes down, not just when capability goes up.

04

The Interface Is the Org Chart

How a tool routes work tells you who has authority. Designing software is, quietly, designing organisational behaviour. Architects know this; product people often forget it.

05

Workflows Reveal Culture

You cannot understand a company’s product priorities by reading its roadmap. You understand them by watching what its top-paid people actually do every day.

06

Build for the 6 PM Manager

Enterprise software is judged at peak load, not in demos. Every screen should answer one question someone needs at 6 PM on a Tuesday, under pressure, with no time to think.

07

Architecture Trains the Eye

Five years of drawing buildings teaches that constraints are generative. Site, brief, code, budget — they don’t limit the design, they are the design. Same rule in software.
